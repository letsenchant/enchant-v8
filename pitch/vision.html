01

Why this works now

You’re free. For the first time in a decade, your craft belongs to you. Every health company’s board is asking for the AI answer, and in consumer health almost nobody selling them help has actually shipped Gen AI products at scale. You have. That intersection, health products, behavior design, and frontier AI, is nearly empty of credible people.

The buyers already exist. Funded health companies post-raise, under pressure to find their next growth engine, who can’t hire a founding-caliber team in time. Two of them tried to hire me this year alone. And it isn’t anecdote: more than half of 2025’s digital-health funding went to AI-enabled companies, and Fitt’s job board lists 48 open AI-titled roles across 23 funded health companies this week. The move is renting us as a pair: something nobody else can offer, because nobody else is us.

And it protects the real bets. The practice is the cash engine; Kasane and Coplay stay the equity engine. Client work funds them, sharpens them, and feeds them. It never eats them.

02

What we sell

The front door

Product + AI Diagnostic

2 weeks · $36k

A two-week read of a company’s product, audience, and AI readiness. Small enough for an easy first yes; designed to grow into the flagship. I can run these mostly solo while you rest.

The flagship

Growth Lab

6–10 weeks · $150–250k

An embedded founding-team pod: one growth bet, thesis to shipped product to first revenue. This is the one that needs us both, and the one that pays for the year.

The long game

AI-Native Operations

Monthly · $15–20k/mo

A standing retainer building a client’s internal AI systems. Steady, calm work that compounds, and a natural continuation after a flagship ends.

03

What one year looks like

The floor

1 flagship + 2 diagnostics · ~10 weeks billed

≈ $222k. One good engagement plus two front-door projects covers our year, at roughly one-fifth of our working time. Everything else stays for the kids, the products, and rest.

The target

2 flagships + 2 diagnostics · 16–24 weeks billed

≈ $372–472k. A strong year. Rebuilds runway and funds the products properly, still under a third of our time.

The ceiling we won’t chase

3+ flagships, back to back

More money, and it eats the products, the family rhythm, and us. We don’t staff this year. That’s a promise, not a forecast.

04

The promises

You rest first

You shape this with me now; your hands join when you’re ready. The diagnostic can run Paul-heavy. Nothing is scheduled against your recovery.

One client at a time

Always. It protects the quality, the kids window, and the products. We say no to the second flagship until the first is done and we’ve had a product block.

The kids window is the trunk

Everything else branches around it. Utilization stays under a third of our time, by design, every scenario.

We can change this

A standing clause between us, reviewed at every CEO review. If it stops serving us, we redesign it or wind it down. No sunk-cost loyalty to a structure.

05

Your part, when you’re ready

Design leadership and the hands-on craft you’ve been missing: research synthesis, product design, the Gen AI patterns you know better than almost anyone selling them. On a flagship we’re a pair: I take strategy, build, and the sales motion; you take the design half whole.

And half the differentiation is simply that you exist. Clients can’t hire a Microsoft Gen AI principal designer. They can rent one, for a season, with me attached.
