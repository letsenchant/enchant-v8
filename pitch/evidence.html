The evidence file.

Every market claim we make, sourced and dated. Pulled June 2026 from primary research, public job boards, and the people running the companies. Judge it yourself.

01

The demand is universal

AI-enabled startups captured 54% of all US digital-health funding in 2025, up from 37% in 2024, across 50% of deals, with a 61% valuation premium at Series C. Total market: $14.2B, +35% year over year.

Rock Health · Jan 2026

Rock Health retired its “AI deal” metric in Q1 2026 because “that distinction is blurring as AI becomes table stakes in how digital health companies and their offerings are built and delivered.” The metric died because the answer became everyone.

Rock Health · Apr 2026

53% of payer and provider C-suite executives call AI an “immediate or urgent priority”, and 73% are growing their financial commitments to it (survey of 60+ executives, including 10 of the top 20 providers).

Define Ventures · Nov 2024

The arc in one stat pair: 6% of health systems had a gen-AI strategy in 2023; 70% of providers and 80% of payers have one in place or in development today.

Bain × KLAS · Oct 2025

AI captured 55% of all health-tech funding in 2025. AI-native health companies run at $500K–$1M+ revenue per employee, against $100–200K for traditional peers.

Bessemer · Jan 2026

44% of wearable owners already use AI chatbots for their health. OpenAI launched ChatGPT Health and Anthropic launched Claude for Healthcare within days of each other; Oura filed its IPO as a “global health intelligence” platform.

Rock Health · 2026 · Fitt Insider Nos. 369–400
02

The execution gap is real

Only ~30% of completed gen-AI proofs-of-concept ever reach production. 45% of use cases sit stuck in ideation. The top cited barrier, by 41–52% of healthcare leaders: lack of in-house AI expertise.

Bessemer × AWS × Bain · Apr 2025

95% of enterprise gen-AI pilots deliver no measurable P&L impact, and tools built with external partners succeed roughly twice as often as internal builds.

MIT NANDA · Aug 2025

In consumer health specifically, the bottleneck isn’t models. Fitt Insider’s editorial line across 2026: “While data is being democratized, action isn’t. Long-term behavior change remains the bottleneck.”

Fitt Insider No. 369 · Jan 2026
03

The hiring census

We counted every posting on jobs.fitt.co, the job board for the health & fitness industry, by hand on June 11, 2026: 1,224 live postings, of which 48 carry AI or ML in the title, across 23 funded health and wellness companies, plus 128 senior product, data, and engineering-leadership searches. Each one is a capability gap a company expects to take months to fill.

  • Komodo Health: “VP, Artificial Intelligence” and “VP, AI Engineering,” open since February 16: a four-month-and-counting search for the AI leadership layer.
  • Headspace: “Director, AI Enablement.” Spring Health: “Director, AI Enablement & Advancement,” posted days ago. PushPress: “Head of Data & AI Enablement.” The recurring word is enablement: hiring someone to teach the company AI.
  • WHOOP: four AI roles at once (design, ML, product marketing, iOS), fresh off a $575M raise at $10B earmarked for clinical AI.
  • Hello Heart: “Senior AI Builder”: a one-person Gen AI shipping role, posted as a job.
  • Sword Health: “Head of Product, Predict” and “Head of Data, Predict”: an AI product line with no leaders for it yet.
  • Grow Therapy, Headway, Twin Health, Charlie Health, Curai, Lantern, knownwell, Practice Better: one to three AI-titled searches each.

05

Read honestly

Three things cut the other way, and we’d rather you hear them from us. Enterprise consultancies do ship Gen AI at scale; Accenture booked $5.9B in gen-AI work in fiscal 2025. That tier is why we scope our claim to consumer health product experiences, where they don’t play. Clinical-operations AI is genuinely working (scribes, billing, triage), so the open lane is the member-facing experience, which happens to be the lane we’ve spent our careers in. And roughly 70% of executives say they’d cut AI spend if returns disappoint this year, which is why every engagement we run is priced against shipped product and first revenue, never against “AI” as an abstraction.
