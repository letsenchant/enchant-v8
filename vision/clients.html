The AI answer, in the product and the operations

Every health company has an AI strategy deck; far fewer have working systems. Rock Health stopped counting “AI deals” this year because AI is now table stakes in how digital health gets built, and the top barrier buyers name is missing in-house expertise. The gap between pilot and platform is execution. External partners ship successful AI roughly twice as often as internal builds.

So every engagement ships both surfaces: the AI-native experience your customers touch, and the internal systems your team runs after we leave.

In your product

AI-native experiences

  • Health coaches and copilots people actually trust and return to
  • Personalization engines on top of your data: labs, wearables, behavior
  • Conversational interfaces designed for health-grade trust
  • The eval suites that keep all of it honest as models change

In your operations

AI-accelerated company

  • Agent workflows for support, operations, and content that reduce busywork
  • Internal harnesses your team extends without us
  • Evaluation and quality systems before automation
  • Hands-on training so the capability stays when we leave

Evals before features

If you can’t measure whether the AI is good, you can’t ship it to people’s health. Quality systems come first, every time.

Harness over hype

The durable value is the scaffolding: the workflows, data plumbing, and evaluation loops that survive every model upgrade.

Your team runs it

Every engagement ends with your people extending the system without us. Systems your team can run, never a dependency you have to keep buying.
